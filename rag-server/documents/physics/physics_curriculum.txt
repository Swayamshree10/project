PHYSICS - ENGINEERING CURRICULUM
==================================

CHAPTER 1: MECHANICS
---------------------

Newton's Laws of Motion:

Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia):
A body remains at rest or in uniform motion in a straight line unless acted upon by an external force.

Newton's Second Law:
The rate of change of momentum of a body is directly proportional to the applied force and takes place in the direction of the force.
Formula: F = ma
Where F = Force (Newtons), m = mass (kg), a = acceleration (m/s²)
Example: A 5kg object accelerates at 3 m/s². Force = 5 × 3 = 15 N

Newton's Third Law:
For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
Example: A rocket expels gas downward (action), the rocket moves upward (reaction).

Work, Energy and Power:
Work = Force × Displacement × cos(θ)
W = Fd cos θ (Joules)
Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv²
Potential Energy: PE = mgh
Power: P = W/t = Fv (Watts)

Conservation of Energy:
Total mechanical energy = KE + PE = constant (in absence of friction)

CHAPTER 2: WAVES AND OPTICS
-----------------------------

Wave Properties:
- Wavelength (λ): distance between two consecutive crests
- Frequency (f): number of waves per second (Hz)
- Wave speed: v = fλ
- Period: T = 1/f

Snell's Law of Refraction:
n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
Where n = refractive index

CHAPTER 3: THERMODYNAMICS
---------------------------

Laws of Thermodynamics:
First Law: Energy cannot be created or destroyed. ΔU = Q - W
Second Law: Heat flows from hot to cold. Entropy of an isolated system always increases.
Third Law: Entropy approaches zero as temperature approaches absolute zero.

Ideal Gas Law:
PV = nRT
Where P = pressure, V = volume, n = moles, R = 8.314 J/mol·K, T = temperature (K)

CHAPTER 4: ELECTROMAGNETISM
-----------------------------

Coulomb's Law:
F = kq₁q₂/r²
k = 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²

Electric Field:
E = F/q = kQ/r²

Ohm's Law:
V = IR
Where V = voltage (Volts), I = current (Amperes), R = resistance (Ohms)

Power in circuits:
P = VI = I²R = V²/R
